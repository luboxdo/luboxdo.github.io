 A PAST EVENT 

The Proposal
In August of 2020, my then-boyfriend, Samuel Boxdorfer, took me on a date.  Underneath a tree, we shared a picnic lunch, this was the same place where he had asked me to be his girlfriend two years prior.  To his surprise, on this date, I told him to propose to me, no ring needed, I just wanted to be his wife.  He laughed and came up with some reason to why he couldn’t propose. I continued to ask him throughout the date, and he got down on one knee and proposed, confessing he had been waiting the whole day for the right moment to ask.

Planning The Wedding


We decided we wanted to get married in January of the next year, leaving us roughly five months to plan our wedding.  Luckily, we knew we wanted a small wedding in my hometown, at my home church.  Within the first month of planning, we had everything set up, the colors would be green and gold, the bridal party would consist of eight friends, our photographer and pastor had been picked out, and everything was going smoothly- until it wasn’t.  Our photographer canceled on us a month before the wedding, luckily a family friend stepped in to fill the position.  On top of that, three days before the wedding, our pastor canceled, he was stuck across the state in a snowstorm.  Luckily, my father was in seminary at the time and was able to get us in contact with a substitute pastor who gladly stepped in.

Our Wedding Day


At Noon on January 2nd, 2021, the wedding began.  My husband, who does not cry often, cried as I walked down the aisle.  The ceremony was a short twenty-five minutes, surrounded by a group of twenty-five people including the pastor and bridal party.  We said our vows, kissed, and walk down the aisle and out the main lobby doors with smiles on our faces.  When we reached the lobby, alone, we both looked at each other and sighed a sigh of relief.  Everything had gone just fine.  Our bridal party, friends, and family all came out to congratulate us on our marriage.  About forty minutes later we were signing our marriage certificate, taking photos, and finally heading to the reception.  Our reception was held at my parent’s local business in Alton- just the bridal party, parents, and us.  We ate a small home-cooked meal, along with our wedding cake, and had a wonderful conversation with the company.  By four o’clock, the reception was over, and we were headed home to our new apartment together for the first time.
